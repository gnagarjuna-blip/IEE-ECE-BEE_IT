1. Series R-L-C Circuit Calculation [Numerical]

A series circuit has R = 10 \Omega, L = 0.1 H, and C = 100 \muF. Calculate the **impedance** and the **power factor** when connected to a 230 V, 50 Hz AC supply.

Suggested Steps for Solution:
  1. Calculate the inductive reactance: **X_L = 2\pi f L** (f=50 Hz, L=0.1 H). (X_L \approx 31.42 \Omega)
  2. Calculate the capacitive reactance: **X_C = 1/(2\pi f C)** (C=100 \times 10^{-6} F). (X_C \approx 31.83 \Omega)
  3. Calculate the net reactance: **X = X_C - X_L** (Since X_C > X_L, the circuit is capacitive). (X \approx 0.41 \Omega)
  4. Calculate the Impedance: **Z = \sqrt{R^2 + X^2}** (\Omega). (Z \approx 10.008 \Omega)
  5. Calculate the Power Factor: **PF = \cos \Phi = R/Z** (Leading). (PF \approx 0.999, Leading)

2. Transformer Turns Calculation (EMF/Turn) [Numerical]

The maximum flux density in the core of 250/3000 Volts 50 Hz single phase transformer is 1.2 Wb/m^2. If the **EMF per turn is 8V**, determine primary and secondary turns.

Suggested Steps for Solution:
  1. **Primary Turns (N_1):** N_1 = V_{primary} / E_{t} = 250 V / 8 V = 31.25 turns. (Round to 31 or 32 turns)
  2. **Secondary Turns (N_2):** N_2 = V_{secondary} / E_{t} = 3000 V / 8 V = 375 turns.

3. Define Voltage Regulation and Efficiency of a Transformer. Give Losses.

A. Voltage Regulation (VR):

**Definition:** The change in secondary terminal voltage from no-load to full-load (primary voltage being constant), expressed as a percentage of the full-load voltage. VR = \frac{V_{2, No-Load} - V_{2, Full-Load}}{V_{2, Full-Load}} \times 100\%.

B. Efficiency (\eta):

**Definition:** The ratio of output power to input power, typically expressed as a percentage. \eta = \frac{\text{Output Power}}{\text{Input Power}} = \frac{\text{Output Power}}{\text{Output Power} + \text{Losses}} \times 100\%.

C. Losses:
  1. **Core (Iron) Losses (P_i):** Constant losses (Hysteresis Loss + Eddy Current Loss).
  2. **Copper Losses (P_{cu}):** Variable losses, proportional to the square of the load current (I^2R losses).

4. Explain about i) Switch Fuse (SFU) ii) Earth Leakage Circuit Breaker (ELCB).

i) Switch Fuse Unit (SFU):

**Definition:** A device that combines a manually operated switch (for isolation/switching) and a fuse (for automatic protection against overcurrent/short circuit). It is used for manually isolating a circuit and providing protection.

ii) Earth Leakage Circuit Breaker (ELCB):

**Definition:** A safety device that monitors the voltage of the protected circuit's metal case relative to earth. It trips the circuit if a significant voltage rise is detected, protecting against indirect contact shock. *Note: ELCBs are largely superseded by RCCBs/RCDs.*

5. Derive the expression for the resonant frequency of a series R-L-C circuit and explain its significance.

A. Derivation:
  1. **Condition for Resonance:** X_L = X_C.
  2. **Substitute Formulas:** 2\pi f_r L = \frac{1}{2\pi f_r C}
  3. **Rearrange:** f_r = \frac{1}{2\pi \sqrt{LC}} \text{ (Hz)}.
B. Significance at Resonance:

The circuit impedance is minimum (Z = R), the current is maximum, and the power factor is unity (\cos\Phi = 1).

6. Derive the EMF equation of a single-phase transformer.

Definition:

The EMF equation of a transformer defines the relationship between the induced voltage (E), the frequency (f), the number of turns (N), and the maximum magnetic flux (\Phi_m).

Suggested Steps for Derivation:
  1. Start with Faraday's Law: e = -N \frac{d\Phi}{dt}.
  2. Assume sinusoidal flux: \Phi = \Phi_{m} \sin(2\pi f t).
  3. Differentiate \Phi to find instantaneous EMF (e).
  4. Find the Maximum EMF (E_{max} = 2\pi f \Phi_{m} N).
  5. Calculate the RMS Value: E_{RMS} = E_{max} / \sqrt{2}.
  6. **Final Result:** E_{RMS} = 4.44 f \Phi_{m} N (Volts).

7. Discuss the construction and working of a three-phase induction motor.

A. Construction:
  1. **Stator:** Fixed outer part with 3-phase windings to produce RMF.
  2. **Rotor:** Moving part (Squirrel Cage or Wound Rotor).
B. Working Principle:
  1. 3-phase supply creates **Rotating Magnetic Field (RMF)** at synchronous speed (N_s).
  2. RMF cuts rotor conductors, **inducing EMF** (Faraday's Law).
  3. Induced EMF causes **current** in the short-circuited rotor.
  4. Current-carrying rotor conductors experience **torque** (Lorentz Force), rotating the rotor (N_r).
  5. **Condition:** N_r < N_s (Slip must exist for induction to occur).

8. Explain the constructional features and working principle of a DC machine.

A. Constructional Features (Key Parts):
  1. **Yoke:** Outer frame (magnetic path).
  2. **Poles & Field Winding:** Creates main flux.
  3. **Armature Core & Winding:** Rotating part (carries current/induces EMF).
  4. **Commutator:** Converts AC induced in armature to DC in the external circuit (Rectification).
B. Working Principle:

Operates based on **Faraday's Law** (Generator mode) or the **Lorentz Force Principle** (Motor mode). The presence of the commutator makes it a DC machine, ensuring unidirectional output/torque.

9. Describe the construction and working principle of a Synchronous Generator.

A machine that produces AC power when driven by a prime mover.

Key Working Point:

The rotor (field winding, DC excited) is rotated at synchronous speed (N_s = 120f/P). The rotating flux cuts the stationary stator (armature) windings, inducing a 3-phase EMF at the supply frequency.

10. Explain the different types of earthing used in electrical installations.

Earthing connects non-current carrying metallic parts of equipment to the earth to ensure safety.

Common Types:
  1. **Pipe Earthing:** A GI or cast iron perforated pipe is buried vertically, surrounded by salt and charcoal for low resistance.
  2. **Plate Earthing:** A copper or GI plate is buried vertically in a deep pit, also surrounded by conductivity-enhancing materials.

11. Describe the construction and operation of a Miniature Circuit Breaker (MCB).

Operation:

An MCB provides dual protection: **Thermal** (using a bimetallic strip for overload) and **Magnetic** (using a solenoid for instantaneous short circuit). It detects and automatically breaks the circuit during faulty conditions.

12. Transformer Calculation: Turns and Core Area [Numerical]

A 4500 V/225 V, 50 Hz single-phase transformer is to have an approximate e.m.f. per turn of 15 V and operate with a maximum flux density (B_m) of 1.4 T. Calculate **(i) the number of primary and secondary turns** and **(ii) the cross-sectional area of the core**.

Solution Steps:
  1. **(i) Turns:** N_1 = 4500/15 = 300 turns. N_2 = 225/15 = 15 turns.
  2. **(ii) Core Area (A_c):** Use E_{RMS}/N = 4.44 f \Phi_m. \Phi_m = (15) / (4.44 \times 50) \approx 0.0675 Wb. Area A_c = \Phi_m / B_m = 0.0675 / 1.4 \approx 0.0482 \text{ m}^2.
