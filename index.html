1. Series R-L-C Circuit Calculation [Numerical]

A series circuit has R = 10 $\Omega$, L = 0.1 H, and C = 100 $\mu$F. Calculate the **impedance** and the **power factor** when connected to a 230 V, 50 Hz AC supply.

Suggested Steps for Solution:
  1. Calculate the inductive reactance: **$X_L = 2\pi f L$** ($f=50 Hz, L=0.1 H$).
  2. Calculate the capacitive reactance: **$X_C = 1/(2\pi f C)$** ($C=100 \times 10^{-6} F$).
  3. Calculate the net reactance: **$X = X_L - X_C$**.
  4. Calculate the Impedance: **$Z = \sqrt{R^2 + X^2}$** ($\Omega$).
  5. Calculate the Power Factor: **$PF = \cos \Phi = R/Z$** (Leading or lagging based on $X_C$ vs $X_L$).

2. Transformer Turns Calculation (EMF/Turn) [Numerical]

The maximum flux density in the core of 250/3000 Volts 50 Hz single phase transformer is 1.2 Wb/m$^2$. If the **EMF per turn is 8V**, determine primary and secondary turns.

Suggested Steps for Solution:
  1. **Formula:** Since EMF per turn ($E_t$) is constant, $E_t = V_1 / N_1 = V_2 / N_2$.
  2. **Primary Turns ($N_1$):** $N_1 = V_{primary} / E_t = 250 V / 8 V$.
  3. **Secondary Turns ($N_2$):** $N_2 = V_{secondary} / E_t = 3000 V / 8 V$.

*(The flux density information is extra data when EMF/turn is given, but useful for calculating core area if required later.)*

3. Define Voltage Regulation and Efficiency of a Transformer. Give Losses.

A. Voltage Regulation (VR):

**Definition:** The change in secondary terminal voltage from no-load to full-load (primary voltage being constant), expressed as a percentage of the full-load voltage. $VR = \frac{V_{2, No-Load} - V_{2, Full-Load}}{V_{2, Full-Load}} \times 100\%$.

B. Efficiency ($\eta$):

**Definition:** The ratio of output power to input power, typically expressed as a percentage. $\eta = \frac{\text{Output Power}}{\text{Input Power}} = \frac{\text{Output Power}}{\text{Output Power} + \text{Losses}} \times 100\%$.

C. Losses:
  1. **Core (Iron) Losses ($P_i$):** Constant losses irrespective of load. Includes Hysteresis Loss and Eddy Current Loss.
  2. **Copper Losses ($P_{cu}$):** Variable losses, proportional to the square of the load current ($I^2R$ losses).

4. Explain about i) Switch Fuse (SFU) ii) Earth Leakage Circuit Breaker (ELCB).

i) Switch Fuse Unit (SFU):

**Definition:** A device that combines an electrical switch (for manual isolation and switching) and a fuse (for automatic protection against overcurrent/short circuit). It is used for manually isolating a circuit and providing protection.

ii) Earth Leakage Circuit Breaker (ELCB):

**Definition:** A safety device that monitors the voltage of the protected circuit's metal case relative to earth. It operates by sensing a potential rise on the protective earth conductor (PE) if a fault occurs, and tripping the circuit. *Note: Modern systems prefer RCCB/RCBO which detect current imbalance.*

5. Describe the Construction and Working Principle of a Synchronous Generator.

A. Construction:

* **Stator (Armature):** Fixed outer part with 3-phase windings where the EMF is induced. * **Rotor (Field):** Inner rotating part fed by DC (exciter) to create the main magnetic field. Can be Salient Pole (for low speed) or Cylindrical/Non-salient Pole (for high speed).

B. Working Principle:
  1. The DC field winding on the rotor is energized, creating a magnetic flux.
  2. A prime mover rotates the rotor at **synchronous speed ($N_s$)**.
  3. As the field poles rotate, the magnetic flux links the stationary 3-phase stator conductors.
  4. According to **Faraday's Law**, an EMF is induced in the stator windings, generating 3-phase AC power.
  5. The rotor speed is synchronized with the frequency ($N_s = 120f/P$).

6. Explain the different types of earthing used in electrical installations.

Brief Definition:

Earthing (or grounding) is the process of connecting the non-current carrying metallic parts of an electrical apparatus or the neutral point of a system to the earth, to provide a low-resistance path for leakage/fault currents and ensure safety.

Types (Common):
  1. **Pipe Earthing:** A galvanized iron (GI) or cast iron perforated pipe is buried vertically in the ground. The area around the pipe is filled with alternate layers of salt and charcoal/coke for lowering the earth resistance.
  2. **Plate Earthing:** A copper or GI plate is buried vertically in a pit more than 3 meters deep. The plate is surrounded by alternate layers of salt and charcoal/coke.
Significance:

It maintains the potential of the system parts relative to the ground, prevents electrocution, and provides a safe path for fault current, enabling protective devices (like ELCB/fuse) to trip.

7. Derive the expression for the resonant frequency of a series R-L-C circuit and explain its significance.

A. Derivation:
  1. **Condition for Resonance:** Resonance occurs in a series RLC circuit when the inductive reactance ($X_L$) equals the capacitive reactance ($X_C$). $$X_L = X_C$$
  2. **Substitute Reactance Formulas:** $$2\pi f_r L = \frac{1}{2\pi f_r C}$$
  3. **Rearrange for $f_r$ (Resonant Frequency):** $$(2\pi f_r)^2 = \frac{1}{LC}$$ $$4\pi^2 f_r^2 = \frac{1}{LC}$$ $$f_r^2 = \frac{1}{4\pi^2 LC}$$
  4. **Final Expression:** $$f_r = \frac{1}{2\pi \sqrt{LC}} \text{ (Hz)}$$
B. Significance at Resonance:

The circuit impedance is minimum ($Z = R$), the current is maximum, and the power factor is unity ($\cos\Phi = 1$).

8. Transformer Turns and Core Area Calculation [Numerical]

A 4500 V/225 V, 50 Hz single-phase transformer is to have an approximate e.m.f. per turn of 15 V and operate with a maximum flux density ($B_m$) of 1.4 T. Calculate **(i) the number of primary and secondary turns** and **(ii) the cross-sectional area of the core**.

Suggested Steps for Solution:
  1. **(i) Primary and Secondary Turns:** $$N_1 = V_1 / (EMF/turn) = 4500 V / 15 V$$ $$N_2 = V_2 / (EMF/turn) = 225 V / 15 V$$
  2. **(ii) Maximum Flux ($\Phi_m$):** Use the EMF equation in terms of $N$: $$E_{RMS} = 4.44 f \Phi_{m} N$$ Since $E_{RMS}/N$ (EMF per turn) is $15 V$: $$\Phi_m = \frac{E_{RMS} / N}{4.44 f} = \frac{15 V}{4.44 \times 50 Hz} \text{ (Webers)}$$
  3. **(iii) Core Area ($A_c$):** Use the relationship between flux, flux density, and area: $$A_c = \Phi_m / B_m = \Phi_m / 1.4 \text{ (m}^2)$$

9. Derive the EMF equation of a single-phase transformer.

Definition:

The EMF equation of a transformer defines the relationship between the induced voltage ($E$), the frequency ($f$), the number of turns ($N$), and the maximum magnetic flux ($\Phi_m$).

Suggested Steps for Derivation:
  1. State **Faraday's Law:** Instantaneous EMF $e = -N \frac{d\Phi}{dt}$.
  2. Assume sinusoidal flux: $\Phi = \Phi_{m} \sin(2\pi f t)$.
  3. Differentiate $\Phi$: $e = -N \frac{d}{dt} (\Phi_{m} \sin(2\pi f t)) = -N (2\pi f \Phi_{m} \cos(2\pi f t))$.
  4. The **Maximum EMF ($E_{max}$)** occurs when $\cos(2\pi f t) = 1$: $E_{max} = 2\pi f \Phi_{m} N$.
  5. Calculate the **RMS Value ($E$):** For a sinusoid, $E_{RMS} = E_{max} / \sqrt{2}$.
  6. **Final Result:** $E_{RMS} = \frac{2\pi}{\sqrt{2}} f \Phi_{m} N \approx 4.44 f \Phi_{m} N$ (Volts).

10. Explain the constructional features and working principle of a DC machine.

A. Constructional Features:
  1. **Yoke:** Outer frame, provides mechanical support and path for magnetic flux.
  2. **Field System (Poles and Field Windings):** Creates the main magnetic flux (electromagnet).
  3. **Armature Core and Winding:** Rotating part where EMF is induced and current is carried.
  4. **Commutator:** Converts the AC induced in the armature winding into DC for the external circuit (Rectification).
  5. **Brushes:** Stationary parts that collect current from the rotating commutator.
B. Working Principle (Motor/Generator):

A DC machine can operate as a **Motor** (Electrical to Mechanical) based on the **Lorentz Force Principle** ($F = B I l$) or as a **Generator** (Mechanical to Electrical) based on **Faraday's Law** ($e = B l v$). The Commutator ensures the output is always DC.

11. Discuss the construction and working of a three-phase induction motor.

A. Construction:
  1. **Stator:** Contains a laminated core with three-phase windings that create a Rotating Magnetic Field (RMF) when supplied with 3-phase AC.
  2. **Rotor:** The moving part. Two main types: Squirrel Cage (conductors shorted by end rings) and Wound Rotor (conductors connected to slip rings).
B. Working Principle:
  1. The 3-phase supply to the stator generates an **RMF** rotating at synchronous speed ($N_s$).
  2. The RMF cuts the stationary rotor conductors, **inducing an EMF** in them (Electromagnetic Induction).
  3. The induced EMF drives a **current** through the short-circuited rotor.
  4. The current-carrying rotor conductors experience a **mechanical force/torque** (Lorentz Force) that causes the rotor to rotate in the direction of the RMF.
  5. The rotor speed ($N_r$) must always be less than $N_s$ (Slip) for continuous EMF induction.

12. Describe the construction and operation of a Miniature Circuit Breaker (MCB).

A. Construction:
  1. **Bimetallic Strip:** Senses and trips on prolonged **overload** current (Thermal action).
  2. **Solenoid/Electromagnet:** Senses and trips instantaneously on large **short circuit** current (Magnetic action).
  3. **Operating Mechanism:** Ensures fast breaking action.
  4. **Arc Chute:** Divides and extinguishes the electric arc created when the contacts open.
B. Operation:

The MCB provides dual protection: **Thermal protection** (slow trip for moderate overcurrent) and **Magnetic protection** (instantaneous trip for high short circuit current).
