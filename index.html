IV. Important Subjective Questions & Study Guide

Brief definitions, steps, or explanation outlines for key long-answer questions.

1. Derive the EMF Equation of a Single-Phase Transformer.

  • **Brief Definition:** The EMF equation relates the voltage induced in the primary and secondary windings to the frequency and maximum flux in the core.
  • **Suggested Steps:**
    1. Start with Faraday's Law of Electromagnetic Induction: $e = -N \frac{d\Phi}{dt}$.
    2. Assume flux ($\Phi$) is sinusoidal: $\Phi = \Phi_{m} \sin(\omega t)$, where $\omega = 2\pi f$.
    3. Differentiate $\Phi$ to find instantaneous EMF ($e$).
    4. Find the maximum value of EMF ($E_{max}$).
    5. Calculate the RMS value ($E$) by dividing $E_{max}$ by $\sqrt{2}$.
    6. Final Result: **$E_{RMS} = 4.44 f \Phi_{m} N$** (where $N$ is the number of turns).

2. Define Voltage Regulation and Efficiency of a Transformer. Give Losses in a Transformer.

  • **Voltage Regulation (VR):** The change in secondary terminal voltage from no-load to full-load (primary voltage being kept constant), expressed as a percentage of the full-load voltage. $$VR = \frac{V_{2, No-Load} - V_{2, Full-Load}}{V_{2, Full-Load}} \times 100\%$$
  • **Efficiency ($\eta$):** The ratio of output power to input power, typically expressed as a percentage. $$\eta = \frac{\text{Output Power}}{\text{Input Power}} = \frac{\text{Output Power}}{\text{Output Power} + \text{Losses}} \times 100\%$$
  • **Losses in Transformer:**
    • **Core (Iron) Losses ($P_i$):** Constant losses irrespective of load.
      • *Hysteresis Loss:* Due to reversal of magnetization in the core.
      • *Eddy Current Loss:* Due to induced currents in the core material.
    • **Copper Losses ($P_{cu}$):** Variable losses, proportional to the square of the load current ($I^2R$ losses).

3. Discuss the Construction and Working of a Three-Phase Induction Motor.

  • **Brief Definition:** A three-phase induction motor is an AC machine that converts electrical power into mechanical power based on the principle of electromagnetic induction.
  • **Construction:**
    • **Stator:** Contains a laminated core with three-phase windings placed in slots. When energized, it produces a rotating magnetic field ($N_s = 120f/P$).
    • **Rotor:** Two main types: Squirrel Cage (robust, inexpensive, copper/aluminum bars shorted by end rings) and Wound Rotor (has slip rings for external resistance control).
  • **Working Principle:**
    1. The three-phase supply creates a Rotating Magnetic Field (RMF) in the stator at synchronous speed ($N_s$).
    2. The RMF cuts the stationary rotor conductors, inducing an EMF (Faraday's Law).
    3. The induced EMF causes current to flow in the short-circuited rotor conductors.
    4. The current-carrying conductors in the magnetic field experience a mechanical force/torque (Lorentz Force), causing the rotor to rotate.
    5. The rotor speed ($N_r$) is *always less than* $N_s$ (a phenomenon called 'Slip') for induction to continuously occur.
